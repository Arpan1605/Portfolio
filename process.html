Process

Show how you think, not only what you made.

This page gives recruiters and teams a fast picture of how you move from problem framing to design decisions. It is especially useful when your projects are still being fully written.

Why this page matters

Many portfolios show only polished screens. This page helps your portfolio feel more thoughtful and credible.

01

Understand the context

Start with the product, the users, the friction points, and the business goal behind the work.

02

Map the journey

Break the experience into tasks, flows, and decision moments that shape whether the product feels intuitive.

03

Design with intent

Use hierarchy, layout, interaction patterns, and visual restraint to reduce confusion and support action.

04

Refine and explain

Iterate, test assumptions, and document the reasoning so the final work feels grounded and defendable.

Principles

A process page becomes stronger when it reflects your actual design values.

Clarity first

Make interfaces easier to understand before making them more expressive.

Design for confidence

Good UX reduces hesitation and helps people feel sure about their next action.

Explain your choices

Case studies become stronger when your decisions are tied to outcomes, not only aesthetics.
